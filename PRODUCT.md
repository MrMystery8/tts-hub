# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People with temporary or permanent voice loss, caregivers, speech and AAC practitioners, and local TTS researchers. The primary mobile workflow must remain usable one-handed and under fatigue; the desktop workflow supports deeper configuration and evidence review.

## Product Purpose

TTS Hub provides local-first speech generation, reusable reference voices, instant replay of saved phrases, persistent job history, and watermark verification. Success means a user can reach familiar speech quickly without surrendering private voice, text, or audio data to a cloud service.

## Brand Personality

Calm, trustworthy, and precise. The interface should feel like dependable assistive equipment: capable without being clinical, and technical without making routine speech feel complicated.

## Anti-references

Avoid decorative AI-dashboard styling, novelty interaction patterns, hidden primary actions, crowded nested cards, and colour-only state communication. Do not make frequently repeated speech depend on reconfiguring a model or waiting for regeneration.

## Design Principles

- Put the fastest everyday communication path first.
- Keep generated audio and saved phrases under explicit user control.
- Reveal technical depth progressively while preserving a predictable default path.
- State local persistence, generation state, and irreversible consequences plainly.
- Keep desktop and mobile behavior consistent even when their information density differs.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use large touch targets, keyboard-visible focus, readable contrast, redundant state cues, low-fatigue copy, and layouts that do not require horizontal scrolling. Treat one-handed mobile use, reduced dexterity, time pressure, and cognitive fatigue as normal operating conditions.
